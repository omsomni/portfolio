OPEN LETTER

Dear President,

Good day, President Marcos! I would like to express my thoughts, opinions, and expectations from such high position in the country, the President.

To start off, I would like to say that being a president must be extremely pressuring and hard. I congratulate the president for winning the election. To file for president is also a very challenging and serious matter, and I commend the President for his bravery. In my opinion, someone who wants to file and become the President, one must be filled with responsibility, critical thinking, opposed to corruption, sympathy and empathy. These traits are what a president should acknowledge and possess. I would like to express my expectations from the president. The poverty, the corruption, the world-wide issues, criminal cases, and murder. These are, for me, what the Philippines are going through. By being the president, I hope that these will be looked upon and give attention, and most importantly solved. It may take time, the amount of money that needs to be invested in solving these issues are huge and should be given a sufficient budget. But as far as I know, the Philippines is in great debt to a country, and thus should be issued and solved immediately in order to create a great and peaceful environment for the Filipinos.

To summarize, I congratulate and commend your bravery. Being a president comes with great responsibility and issues that needs to be solved within and outside the government. I, as a Filipino, expect the President and the government to solve these issues alongside the many different matters in the economy. Thank you and have a good day!

      Every country needs rules and regulations to keep a normal and peaceful community. These rules and regulations should be mandatory to avoid any harm to its people. Thus, everyone should abide by their country’s rules and regulations. However, not everyone is willing to, therefore the government provides service and assistance in monitoring people’s misbehavior and provide support for the people that need protection. Through the government, the police department was made. The Philippine National Police’s motto, which is “To Serve and to Protect,” has given the community a chance to create an environment that everyone can feel protected, feel safe, and protected. Protection is what the PNP aims to fill in times of situations that need much more than safety.

      The main aim of the police is to protect the nation from different kinds of wrongdoings. The PNP’s police force and some of its actions are documented which can be found on the internet. Some examples are provided below, which are scaled with 1, being highly inefficient, to 10, being highly efficient, that are based on how the police officers fulfilled their duty and service on our society.
      The first news article cited is a manila cop that is facing criminal and admin cases over killing of a delivery rider. The article talks about the police report made about the homicide case. The cop involved is friends with the victim, who were waiting outside of a barangay hall when suddenly the cop was playing with his gun and allegedly went off and hit the victim. This caused a stir on the department, as the cops’ irresponsible gun handling led to a person’s death, due to the police officer’s mishandling, this case is given a 1, which is highly inefficient.
      The second report is about a police officer that quickly responded to a robbery incident. According to the report, a complaint from a student victim, the Buhangin, Davao City responded immediately. An 18-year-old female was walking on September 24, when a motorcycle with two male suspects stopped and pointed an unknown caliber pistol at her and declared a hold up. The student’s bag containing one cellphone worth ₱7,000 and cash amounting to ₱1,570 and other personal belongings was taken. The police ended up chasing the robbers, however, one suspect was able to evade arrest. The back rider of the motorcycle fired his homemade revolver towards the police which the police returned fire to. The injured suspect that was shot by the police was immediately rushed to the hospital but was later declared dead on arrival, the suspect’s identity is still unknown. Although the police were able to chase and catch a suspect, the other was able to run and evade his/her arrest. Considering the injured suspect died due to them firing a pistol towards the officers, and the officers had no other choice but to fire back. The efficiency of the police, in this case, is 7.
      The third and last report is about the police seizing millions of drugs, mainly shabu and dried marijuana, in a single day. According to the report, ₱4.5 million worth of shabu were collected in a series of anti-illegal drugs operations on Saturday, September 24, 2022. Aside from shabu, the cops confiscated 138 bricks and 6 tubular forms of dried marijuana leaves in the Kalinga province, resulting in a total estimated value of ₱17,280,000. Due to this being done within a day, this case is given a 10, which is highly efficient.

      Based on these three reports about the efficiency of the Philippine National Police in fulfilling their functions in our society, the average of their efficiency is 6 from the scale of 1 (being highly inefficient) to 10 (being highly efficient.) The three cases mentioned above are just examples and is subjective to what the police can do. Police authorities can and will still be able to act in accordance with their motto, “To Serve and to Protect.”
